Nature's Decoherence Shield

The source for DNA's different nucleic acids (ATGCs) and its helix structure has been known for years, but the encoding mechanism through which it is created has been a bit of a mystery. Recently, researchers have been theorized that quantum entanglement would have to be the way in which this encoding and structure development is happening at such a rapid rate in such a small space. With studies proving this, DNA must have a decoherence shield.

Superconducting Potential

DNA has long been thought to only serve as a data storage mechanism. However, recent reserach has found that DNA can actually serve as an important means of energy processing. The likelihood of this occuring within living cells at scale is still being determined, but its use for energy transmission could render it a superconductor of sorts.

Tools Already Exist

By connecting what has already been discovered in the editing of proteins to write our own DNA, electron emission technology used within microscopes, the quantum nature of DNA, and the knowledge that exists about constructing quantum virtual machines and quantum processing toolkits, developing the right equipment to utilize DNA as a quantum simulation medium is an interdisciplinary effort that could happen very soon. Currently, Iff's work is focused on discovering how to control quantum mechana within DNA.
